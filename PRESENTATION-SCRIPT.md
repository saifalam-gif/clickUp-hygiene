## Opening (Before Slide 1)

*[When everyone is in the room]*

**"Thank you all for making time today. I know this is an important topic for the organization, and we're grateful to have PnC and leadership in the room. We're here to share where we are with ClickUp, the outcome we're aiming for, and to ask for your partnership to get the whole company aligned on one system. I'll walk through the deck, and we'll do a short hands-on activity. Please interrupt anytime if you have questions."**

*[Pause. Then advance to Slide 1.]*

---

## Slide 1 — Title

**"This is ClickUp: One Source of Truth. We're asking for PnC's collaboration to align the organization on a single system for managing and maintaining tasks — and to launch it company-wide together."**

*[Hold for 3–4 seconds. Let the ask land.]*

---

## Slide 2 — Agenda

**"Here's what we'll cover. We'll start with where we are today and the problem we're solving, then the outcome we want. I'll explain why we need PnC on board and what we're proposing. After that, we'll walk through key ClickUp sections — Inbox, Replies, My Tasks, and Channels — with best practices. Then we'll do a short activity together, and end with next steps."**

*[Brief pause. "Any questions before we dive in?" If no, advance.]*

---

## Slide 3 — Where We Are Today

**"Where we are today: ClickUp is our designated project and task management tool, but usage is fragmented and inconsistent. That hurts collaboration, creates data blind spots, and reduces operational efficiency. We want one shared system that everyone uses properly."**

*[Pause. This sets the stakes.]*

---

## Slide 4 — Current Issues

**"These are the main issues we're seeing. The platform is often treated as optional — teams don't consistently log or maintain tasks. We lack visibility on who is doing what, so we can't properly reward those putting in the work. Heavy contributors go unrecognized — high performers who take on more are invisible; we can't distinguish who is consistently delivering versus who is flying under the radar. There's no bandwidth clarity — we can't see who's overloaded versus who has capacity, so we can't identify who needs to ease off before burnout sets in. Work distribution is opaque — we can't balance load fairly or redistribute when someone is stretched thin. And there's silent overload — people often don't speak up until they're already at breaking point; we lack early signals to intervene. There's no common structure for organizing work. Performance and L&D aren't linked to how people use the system, so motivation to maintain standards is low. And onboarding and mobility suffer because there's no consistent way to hand over work."**

*[Go through each bullet. Emphasize: "These aren't tool problems — they're adoption and governance problems."]*

---

## Slide 5 — Intended Outcome

**"Where we want to be: a single source of truth — one system for all project and task management. Clear work visibility so we can reward those putting in the work. Contributors recognized and rewarded fairly — high performers visible, so recognition and development opportunities go to the right people. Bandwidth clarity — so we see who has capacity and who needs to ease off to avoid burnout. Fair work distribution — load balancing based on actual capacity; we can redistribute when someone is stretched thin. Proactive support before burnout — early visibility into overload so we can intervene before people hit a breaking point. Sustainable workload — the system supports a sustainable pace, not hero culture; we know who needs to go a bit easier. BAU and OKRs in one place. High engagement — tasks updated, communication happening in the platform. Data-driven performance, and consistent structure so teams can collaborate and roles can be handed over easily."**

*[Pause. "This is the north star we're aiming for."]*

---

## Slide 6 — Why We Need PnC on Board

**"To get the whole organization aligned, we need a strong partner in Phase 1. We are asking PnC to collaborate with us — so we can guide you to manage and maintain tasks in a proper, consistent system, and then roll it out company-wide. Phase 1 is with PnC: we define standards, align on structure, and support training and adoption. Phase 2 is company-wide: once we've stabilized with PnC, we launch the same way of working across the organization."**

*[This is the core ask. Speak clearly and confidently. Pause after "company-wide."]*

---

## Slide 7 — How We'll Work With You

**"What we're proposing: we'll guide you to use and maintain the platform consistently — without changing your core structure. We'll agree on standards for spaces, folders, and lists. We'll focus on task management habits: keeping tasks up to date, staying on top of updates and replies. We'll support with training and materials so PnC can own or co-own rollout. And we'll align with your existing cadences and L&D frameworks so the system supports how you already evaluate and develop people."**

*[Emphasize: "We're not imposing a new structure — we're helping you use what you have, better."]*

---

## Slide 8 — ClickUp Sections & Best Practices (Transition)

**"Now we want to walk through some key ClickUp sections: what they are, how to use them, and best practices to keep your work visible and your system clean. We'll cover Inbox, Replies, My Tasks, and Channels — the core areas that support the standards we've discussed."**

*[This bridges from the proposal to the hands-on content.]*

---

## Slide 9 — Inbox: What It Is

**"Section 1: Inbox. The Inbox is your go-to place for task updates — not for chat or email. Many people mistake it for a general inbox. In ClickUp, it's where you see activity on your tasks: status changes, assignments, comments, and updates, all in one feed."**

---

## Slide 10 — Inbox: Where to Find It

**"Here's where you find it — in the left sidebar under Home. The main area shows your task updates with tabs and filters."**

*[If screenshot is missing, say: "You'll see this in the live platform — Inbox is under Home in the sidebar."]*

---

## Slide 11 — Inbox: What Each Tab Means

**"The four tabs: Primary for high-priority updates, Other for the rest — and that's where clutter builds if you don't clear it. Later for items you've deferred, Cleared for what you've processed. Use the tabs to triage: keep Primary for what needs action now, move the rest to Later or Cleared."**

---

## Slide 12 — Inbox: How to Clean It

**"How to clean it: use Filter to narrow by type or time range. Use Clear all to mark items as read, or the blue Clear on each row. Move items to Later if you don't need them now. And review regularly — daily or weekly — so Other doesn't grow into dozens of unread items."**

---

## Slide 13 — Inbox: How to Use It Properly

**"Best practices: treat it as your task activity feed, not messages or email. Check it when you start work. Clear or move items after you've acted on them. And combine it with Assigned Comments and My Tasks for the full picture."**

---

## Slide 14 — Replies: What It Is

**"Section 2: Replies. Replies shows all replies to ClickUp Chat threads you follow, in one place. It's part of ClickUp Chat — not task comments or Inbox. Use it to stay on top of thread conversations so nothing gets missed."**

---

## Slide 15 — Replies: Where to Find It

**"You find it in the Home sidebar. The main area shows Unread and Read tabs. 'You're all caught up' means no unread thread replies."**

---

## Slide 16 — Replies: What Each Tab Means

**"Unread is for threads you haven't read yet — open a thread and it moves to Read. You can Keep unread or Mark unread to triage. Aim for 'You're all caught up' on Unread."**

---

## Slide 17 — Replies: How to Use It Properly

**"Check Replies when you start work, with Inbox. Open threads from Unread to read them. Use Keep unread or Mark unread to triage. And remember: Replies is for Chat threads. For task comments and activity, use Inbox and Assigned Comments."**

---

## Slide 18 — My Tasks: What It Is

**"Section 3: My Tasks. It's your central hub to view and manage work assigned to you. You see Recents, Agenda, My Work, and Assigned to me. Use it daily so your workload stays visible."**

---

## Slide 19 — My Tasks: Where to Find It

**"It's in the sidebar under Home. You can select Assigned to me, Today & Overdue, or Personal List. The main area shows your greeting, Recents, Agenda, My Work, and the Assigned to me task list."**

---

## Slide 20 — My Tasks: Assigned to Me

**"Assigned to me is the most important view. It shows all tasks assigned to you, sorted by due date. You can create tasks, update status, filter, group, and search. Use Filters to narrow by date, assignee, status, priority. Today & Overdue gives you a quick view of what's due. Personal List is your own to-do list."**

---

## Slide 21 — My Tasks: How to Use It Properly

**"Start the day in Assigned to me. Use Group and Filter to focus by status, priority, or due date. Keep Today & Overdue count low. And combine with Inbox and Assigned Comments so nothing gets missed."**

---

## Slide 22 — Channels: What It Is

**"Section 4: Channels. All Channels is where you see ClickUp Chat channels and spaces — team channels, project spaces. You can see which channels you follow, follower counts, and when they were last updated. Use it to manage your subscriptions so you stay in channels that matter and leave ones that don't."**

---

## Slide 23 — Channels: Where to Find It

**"It's in the sidebar under Home. The main area shows channels with Topic, Followers, Last updated, and Following status. You can create channels and search."**

---

## Slide 24 — Channels: Best Practices

**"Best practices: don't stay in channels you don't need — unfollow channels that are no longer relevant. Review regularly using Followers and Last updated to spot inactive channels. Use Favorites for what matters daily. And leave when projects end — unfollow so your Replies and notifications stay focused."**

---

## Slide 25 — ClickUp Hygiene

**"One ongoing habit we want to establish: ClickUp hygiene. You will all have a Single Source of Truth — an SSoT — on the task list. Every Friday before signing out, team leads should clean their ClickUp tasks, show their screen, and check that the numbers are in a positive way. Before leaving, verify: tasks completed, tasks have assignees, overdue count is low or zero, Stuck and Blocked are minimal, and team members' inbox unread counts are low. This keeps the system up to date, sets the example for the team, and ensures nothing slips through over the weekend. Here's the SSoT dashboard — check Total Tasks, Tasks Overdue, Task Done, Workload by Status, and Who is behind. Aim for numbers in a positive way before you sign out."**

*[Show the SSoT dashboard screenshot. If doing a live demo, share your ClickUp screen and walk through the dashboard metrics.]*

---

## Slide 26 — Activity: Let's Do It Together

**"Let's do an activity together. Using what we've covered, we'll walk through a practical exercise. Keep in mind: the fragmented usage we want to fix, the single source of truth we're aiming for, and the standards we've discussed. We'll use the screenshots and examples from Inbox, Replies, My Tasks, and Channels as your guide. Follow along as we go."**

*[Pause. Run the activity — e.g. have everyone open ClickUp and triage their Inbox, or review their Assigned to me. Keep it to 5–10 minutes. If time is short, say: "We can do this as a follow-up — the key is applying what we've covered."]*

## Slide 26 — Conclusion & Next Steps

**"To close: we've shared the problem, the outcome we want, and a path that starts with your partnership. We are asking for PnC's collaboration — so we can guide you to manage and maintain tasks in a proper system, align the organization with you, and then launch it company-wide. The next step is to align on scope, timeline, and how we work together — training, communication, and rollout. Thank you for your time and consideration. We look forward to partnering with you."**

*[Pause. Make eye contact. Then:]*

**"I'm happy to take questions."**

## Q&A Preparation

### Likely Questions

1. **"What's the timeline?"**  
   *"We'd like to align with you on that. Phase 1 with PnC could be 4–8 weeks depending on scope. Phase 2 company-wide would follow once we've stabilized."*

2. **"What resources do you need from us?"**  
   *"A committed partner in PnC — someone who can own or co-own adoption, attend training, and help us refine the approach before we roll out."*

3. **"How does this fit with our existing L&D and performance frameworks?"**  
   *"We'll align with your cadences. The goal is to make ClickUp usage visible so it can support how you already evaluate and develop people — not add a new layer."*

4. **"What if teams push back?"**  
   *"That's why we're starting with PnC. If we get it right here, we have a strong reference for the rest of the organization. We'll support with training and materials."*

5. **"What's the ROI?"**  
   *"Visibility, fewer blind spots, faster onboarding, and a foundation for data-driven decisions. The cost of fragmentation is harder to quantify, but we've all felt it."*
