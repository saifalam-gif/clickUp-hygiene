Where we are today

ClickUp is the organization's designated project and task management tool, but usage is fragmented and inconsistent.

This hinders collaboration, creates data blind spots, and reduces operational efficiency. We want one shared system that everyone uses properly.

Current issues

  • Platform seen as optional — Teams treat adherence as non-essential, so tasks and updates are not consistently logged or maintained.
  • No visibility on who is doing what — Work and ownership are unclear; we cannot easily see who is responsible for what or how work is progressing.
  • No common structure — There is no agreed way to organize lists, folders, or spaces, so we lack a reliable data foundation.
  • Performance and L&D not linked — Using the platform well is not yet tied to how people are evaluated or developed, so motivation to maintain standards is low.
  • Onboarding and mobility suffer — Without consistent structure, new joiners and role changes lack clear, role-ready ways to work in the system.

Where we want to be

Intended outcome

  • Single source of truth — One system for all project and task management; no work in silos.
  • Clear visibility on who is doing what — Tasks, assignments, and progress are visible in the platform so we know who owns what and how work is moving.
  • BAU and OKRs in one place — Day-to-day work and objectives tracked in the platform so we measure outcomes, not just activity.
  • High engagement in the system — Tasks updated, communication and replies happening in the platform so nothing is lost.
  • Data-driven performance — How people use and maintain the system is visible and can be tied to performance and development.
  • Consistent structure — Standard ways of organizing work so teams can collaborate and roles can be handed over or scaled easily.

We are asking for your collaboration

Why we need PnC on board

To get the whole organization aligned and into one system, we need a strong partner in Phase 1. We are asking PnC to collaborate with us so we can guide you to manage and maintain tasks in a proper, consistent system — and then roll it out company-wide.

  • Phase 1 — With PnC — We work with you to define standards, align on how tasks and work are structured, and support training and adoption. We will guide you so your team can own and maintain the system in practice.
  • Phase 2 — Company-wide — Once we have aligned and stabilized the system with PnC, we launch the same way of working across the organization so everyone is on one source of truth.

What we're proposing

How we'll work with you

We will guide you to use and maintain the platform in a consistent way — without changing the core structure (e.g. how workspaces, spaces, folders, and lists are used).

  • Standards and structure — Agree one way to organize work (e.g. spaces, folders, lists) so the system is clear and repeatable.
  • Task management and communication — We will guide your team on core habits: keeping tasks up to date, staying on top of updates and replies, and using the platform as the place where work is visible and accountable.
  • Training and materials — We support with training and simple materials so PnC can own or co-own rollout and ongoing adherence.
  • Governance — We align with your existing cadences and L&D/performance frameworks so the system supports how you already evaluate and develop people.

After launch

Once the system is in place and used consistently, we can extend it: link usage to performance and L&D, use data for insights and prioritization, and create templates for onboarding and mobility — all on the same foundation.

ClickUp sections & best practices

Now we want to walk through some key ClickUp sections: what they are, how to use them, and best practices to keep your work visible and your system clean.

We'll cover Inbox, Replies, My Tasks, and Channels — the core areas that support the standards and habits we've discussed.

Section 1

Inbox: What it is

The Inbox is your go-to place for task updates, not for chat or email messages.

Many people mistake it for a general "inbox" (like email or DMs). In ClickUp, the Inbox is where you see activity on your tasks: status changes, assignments, comments, and updates, all in one feed.

Inbox: Where to find it & what you see

Inbox: What each tab means

  • Primary — Most important or high-priority updates.
  • Other — Remaining notifications (e.g. "88 unread"). This is where clutter builds if you don't clear it.
  • Later — Items you've deferred to review later.
  • Cleared — Notifications you've already processed or dismissed.

Use the tabs to triage: keep Primary for what needs action now, move the rest to Later or Cleared so your Inbox stays manageable.

Inbox: How to clean it

  • Filter — Use the Filter button to show only certain types of updates or time ranges. Click the Filter button below the tabs.
  • Clear all — Use "Clear all" (top right) to mark all visible items as read. Use the blue Clear on a row to clear that item only.
  1. Use Later — Move items you don't need now to "Later" so they don't clutter Primary or Other.
  2. Review regularly — Check Inbox daily or weekly so "Other" doesn't grow into dozens of unread items.

Inbox: How to use it properly

  • Treat it as your task activity feed, not as messages or email.
  • Check it when you start work to see what changed on your tasks overnight.
  • Clear or move items after you've acted on them so the count stays low.
  • Combine with Assigned Comments and My Tasks in the sidebar for a full picture of what needs your attention.

Section 2

Replies: What it is

Replies shows all replies to ClickUp Chat threads you follow in one place.

It's part of ClickUp Chat (built-in messaging, channels, and threads), not task comments or Inbox. You see it in the Home sidebar. Use it to stay on top of thread conversations so nothing gets missed and your unread count stays manageable.

Replies: Where to find it & what you see

Replies: What each tab means

  • Unread — All unread replies to Chat threads you follow. Open a thread to read it; it moves to Read. You can "Keep unread" on a thread if you need to return to it.
  • Read — Thread replies you've already read. Use "Read old replies" to open older threads. You can "Mark unread" to bring a thread back to Unread.

Aim for "You're all caught up" on Unread. Check Replies regularly so your Chat thread follow-ups don't pile up.

Replies: How to use it properly

  • Check Replies when you start work (with Inbox) to stay on top of Chat thread follow-ups.
  • Open threads from Unread to read them; they move to Read and your unread count goes down.
  • Use "Keep unread" or "Mark unread" to triage threads. Use "Read old replies" to catch up on older conversations.
  • Replies is for Chat threads. For task comment assignments and activity, use Inbox and Assigned Comments in the sidebar.

Section 3

My Tasks: What it is

My Tasks (formerly Home) is your central hub to view and manage work assigned to you.

It's a canvas where you see cards like Recents, Agenda, My Work, and Assigned to me. Open it from the sidebar under Home. Use it daily so your workload stays visible and nothing slips through.

My Tasks: Where to find it & what you see

My Tasks: Assigned to me and other subsections

Assigned to me (most important)

Shows all tasks assigned to you, sorted by due date. Create tasks (they're auto-assigned to you), update status, edit, filter, group, and search. Use "Show closed" to see completed tasks. Group by status (e.g. In Progress, Blocked, Done) to prioritize.

Filter option: Click Filters (top right of the Assigned to me card) to open the Filters panel. Narrow the list by start date, due date, assignee, status, priority, and more. You can add multiple filters, use nested filters, and save filter sets for quick reuse.

  • Today & Overdue — Quick view of tasks due today and overdue (count in sidebar).
  • Personal List — Your own to-do list separate from the rest of the Hierarchy.

My Work card (on the page) breaks your to-dos into Today, Overdue, Next, and Unscheduled under the To Do tab; use Done and Delegated for completed and delegated items.

My Tasks: How to use it properly

  • Start the day in Assigned to me to see your full workload and update statuses (e.g. In Progress, Done).
  • Use the Assigned to me Group and Filter to focus by status, priority, or due date.
  • Keep Today & Overdue count low; move overdue items to Done or reschedule them.
  • Combine with Inbox and Assigned Comments so task updates and comment action items don't get missed.

Section 4

Channels: What it is

All Channels is where you see ClickUp Chat channels and spaces — team channels, project spaces, and DMs.

You can see which channels you follow, how many followers each has, and when they were last updated. Use it to manage your channel subscriptions so you stay in channels that matter and leave ones that don't.

Channels: Where to find it & what you see

Channels: Best practices

  • Don't stay in channels you don't need — Unfollow channels that are no longer relevant to your role, completed projects, or inactive spaces. A long Following list creates noise and makes it harder to focus.
  • Review regularly — Use the Followers and Last updated columns to spot channels that are inactive or low-value. Consider unfollowing if a channel hasn't been updated in weeks or has few active participants.
  • Use Favorites for what matters — Pin the channels you use daily to Favorites so they're easy to reach. Keep the rest in All Channels and only follow what you need.
  • Leave when projects end — When a project or initiative wraps up, unfollow the channel so your Replies and notifications stay focused on current work.
