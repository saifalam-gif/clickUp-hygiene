ClickUp Hygiene Campaign

Structure, visibility, and clean workflows for the whole organization.

Why this campaign?

We use ClickUp to manage teams, departments, and people, but without a clear structure and top-level visibility, it’s hard to see what’s happening across the organization.

This campaign walks you through specific features: what they are, how to use them, and how to keep your ClickUp clean and structured.

Section 1

Inbox: What it is

The Inbox is your go-to place for task updates, not for chat or email messages.

Many people mistake it for a general “inbox” (like email or DMs). In ClickUp, the Inbox is where you see activity on your tasks: status changes, assignments, comments, and updates, all in one feed.

Inbox: Where to find it & what you see

Inbox: What each tab means

  • Primary - Most important or high-priority updates.
  • Other - Remaining notifications (e.g. “88 unread”). This is where clutter builds if you don’t clear it.
  • Later - Items you’ve deferred to review later.
  • Cleared - Notifications you’ve already processed or dismissed.

Use the tabs to triage: keep Primary for what needs action now, move the rest to Later or Cleared so your Inbox stays manageable.

Inbox: How to clean it

  • Filter - Use the Filter button to show only certain types of updates or time ranges. Click the Filter button below the tabs.
  • Clear all - Use “Clear all” (top right) to mark all visible items as read. Use the blue Clear on a row to clear that item only.
  1. Use Later - Move items you don’t need now to “Later” so they don’t clutter Primary or Other.
  2. Review regularly - Check Inbox daily or weekly so “Other” doesn’t grow into dozens of unread items.

Inbox: How to use it properly

  • Treat it as your task activity feed, not as messages or email.
  • Check it when you start work to see what changed on your tasks overnight.
  • Clear or move items after you’ve acted on them so the count stays low.
  • Combine with Assigned Comments and My Tasks in the sidebar for a full picture of what needs your attention.

Section 2

Replies: What it is

Replies shows all replies to ClickUp Chat threads you follow in one place.

It's part of ClickUp Chat (built-in messaging, channels, and threads), not task comments or Inbox. You see it in the Home sidebar. Use it to stay on top of thread conversations so nothing gets missed and your unread count stays manageable.

Replies: Where to find it & what you see

Replies: What each tab means

  • Unread - All unread replies to Chat threads you follow. Open a thread to read it; it moves to Read. You can "Keep unread" on a thread if you need to return to it.
  • Read - Thread replies you've already read. Use "Read old replies" to open older threads. You can "Mark unread" to bring a thread back to Unread.

Aim for "You're all caught up" on Unread. Check Replies regularly so your Chat thread follow-ups don't pile up.

Replies: How to use it properly

  • Check Replies when you start work (with Inbox) to stay on top of Chat thread follow-ups.
  • Open threads from Unread to read them; they move to Read and your unread count goes down.
  • Use "Keep unread" or "Mark unread" to triage threads. Use "Read old replies" to catch up on older conversations.
  • Replies is for Chat threads. For task comment assignments and activity, use Inbox and Assigned Comments in the sidebar.

Section 3

My Tasks: What it is

My Tasks (formerly Home) is your central hub to view and manage work assigned to you.

It's a canvas where you see cards like Recents, Agenda, My Work, and Assigned to me. Open it from the sidebar under Home. Use it daily so your workload stays visible and nothing slips through.

My Tasks: Where to find it & what you see

My Tasks: Assigned to me and other subsections

Assigned to me (most important)

Shows all tasks assigned to you, sorted by due date. Create tasks (they're auto-assigned to you), update status, edit, filter, group, and search. Use "Show closed" to see completed tasks. Group by status (e.g. In Progress, Blocked, Done) to prioritize.

Filter option: Click Filters (top right of the Assigned to me card) to open the Filters panel. Narrow the list by start date, due date, assignee, status, priority, and more. You can add multiple filters, use nested filters, and save filter sets for quick reuse.

  • Today & Overdue - Quick view of tasks due today and overdue (count in sidebar).
  • Personal List - Your own to-do list separate from the rest of the Hierarchy.

My Work card (on the page) breaks your to-dos into Today, Overdue, Next, and Unscheduled under the To Do tab; use Done and Delegated for completed and delegated items.

My Tasks: How to use it properly

  • Start the day in Assigned to me to see your full workload and update statuses (e.g. In Progress, Done).
  • Use the Assigned to me Group and Filter to focus by status, priority, or due date.
  • Keep Today & Overdue count low; move overdue items to Done or reschedule them.
  • Combine with Inbox and Assigned Comments so task updates and comment action items don't get missed.

Thank you

Use Inbox, Replies, and My Tasks every day to keep your work visible and your ClickUp clean.
